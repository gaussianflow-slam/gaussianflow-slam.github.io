Abstract

Gaussian splatting has recently gained traction as a compelling image representation for SLAM systems, enabling dense and photo-realistic scene modeling. However, its application to monocular SLAM remains challenging due to the lack of reliable geometric cues from a single camera. Without explicit supervision, mapping or tracking could fall in local-minima, resulting in structure-degenerate and inaccurate 3DGS.

To address this challenge, we propose GaussianFlow SLAM, a monocular 3DGS SLAM that leverages optical flow as a geometry-aware cue to guide the optimization of both the projected scene structure and camera poses. By encouraging the projected motion of each Gaussian, termed GaussianFlow, to align with the optical flow, our method introduces consistent structure cues for iterative 3DGS map reconstruction and pose estimation. Furthermore, we introduce normalized error-based densification and pruning modules that refine inactive and unstable Gaussians, thereby contributing to improving map quality and pose accuracy.

Experiments conducted on public datasets demonstrate that our method achieves superior rendering quality and tracking accuracy compared with state-of-the-art algorithms.
